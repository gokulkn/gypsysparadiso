Secrets of the
Shola Forest.

← Back to all stories

Munnar is beautiful, but it's also crowded. If you're willing to travel just 45km further into the mountains, you'll find Vattavada—a place where silence is still a luxury. Here are 5 hidden gems that most tourists miss.

1. Chilanthiyar Falls

Hidden deep within the forest, this tiered waterfall is a secret kept by the locals. It requires a short trek to reach, but the sight of the pristine water cascading down the rocks is worth every step. Unlike the popular waterfalls in Munnar, you might just have this one all to yourself.

2. Pampadum Shola National Park

The smallest national park in Kerala, but perhaps the most beautiful. "Pampadum Shola" translates to "the forest where the snake dances." It's a haven for biodiversity, home to the endangered Nilgiri Marten and elusive leopards. The drive through this park is an experience in itself.

3. The Dolmens of Marayoor

Step back in time—literally. These ancient burial chambers date back to the Stone Age. Perched on a rocky hill, they offer a panoramic view of the Pambar River and the sandalwood forests. It's a hauntingly beautiful spot that feels otherworldly.

4. Vattavada Strawberry Farms

Did you know Vattavada is the "Market of Kerala"? The cool climate here allows for the cultivation of strawberries, apples, and winter vegetables. Visit a local farm, pluck fresh red strawberries right off the plant, and taste the sweetness of the high range.

5. Top Station (The Sunrise View)

Okay, this one is popular, but here's the secret: Go for sunrise, not sunset. Most tourists visit in the evening. At 6 AM, you're above the clouds, watching the sun paint the sky in hues of orange and purple, with the valley of Tamil Nadu stretching out below you.
